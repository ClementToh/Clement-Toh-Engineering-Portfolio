About Me

I am a Mechanical Engineering undergraduate pursuing my Bachelor of Engineering with Honours at the National University of Singapore (NUS)[cite: 2]. My core focus lies at the intersection of high-precision mechanical design and real-time industrial data pipelines.

My foundational engineering journey began at Singapore Polytechnic, where I graduated with Merit in Mechanical Engineering[cite: 2]. Currently, I am engineering advanced motion and vision control systems for laser micro-processing at JD Union, actively spearheading the hardware-software integration of galvanometer scanners and multi-axis motion stages to optimize laser 3D printing[cite: 2].

Previously, at the A*STAR Advanced Remanufacturing and Technology Centre (ARTC), I built an end-to-end industrial data telemetry pipeline using InfluxDB and Grafana to monitor Laser Powder Bed Fusion (LPBF) processes[cite: 2]. Whether I am developing complex joint mechanisms for anthropomorphic exoskeletons or routing custom sensor instrumentation for IIoT devices[cite: 2], my goal is always to build systems that are both mechanically robust and comprehensively data-driven.
